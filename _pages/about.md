Dr. Hui Pan received his B.Sc. in Applied Physics in 2018 and Ph.D. in Physics in 2023 from Hunan University. He mainly focuses on nanoscale quantum transport and energy conversion, and is specialized in theoretical and numerical modelling of transport phenomena in quantum many-body systems. Currently, as a postdoc at National University of Singapore, he works on near-field transfer of heat, momentum, and angular momentum induced by quantum fluctuations. In the doctoral period, he developed ab initio methods for coupled magnon-phonon thermal transport in magnetic nanomaterials. He has authored 19 research papers (as of Jun 2026) with over 500 citations and serves as a reviewer for several well-known physics journals.
