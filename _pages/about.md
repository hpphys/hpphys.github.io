Dr. Hui Pan received his B.Sc. in Applied Physics in 2018 and Ph.D. in Physics in 2023 from Hunan University. He mainly focuses on nanoscale quantum transport and energy conversion, and is specialized in theoretical modelling of transport phenomena in quantum many-body systems and the development of numerical algorithms. In the doctoral period, he developed ab initio methods for coupled magnon-phonon thermal transport in magnetic nanomaterials. Currently, as a postdoc at National University of Singapore, he studies radiative heat, momentum, and angular momentum transfer mediated by nonequilibrium quantum fluctuations, such as Casimir effects and near-field heat radiation. He has authored 19 research papers (as of Jun 2026) with over 500 citations and serves as a reviewer for several well-known physics journals.
